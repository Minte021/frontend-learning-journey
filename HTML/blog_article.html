First Heading

The silent, deep-sea world of the Abyssal Zone remains largely unexplored, acting as a final frontier for marine biology. Organisms here thrive in total darkness under immense pressure, often relying on chemosynthesis rather than sunlight to survive. Recent submersible missions have revealed bizarre, bioluminescent creatures that challenge our understanding of life's adaptability, according to a research article on Oceanography.
